# Property Health Record (PHR) System

## Overview
The Property Health Record (PHR) system monitors and maintains the health status of properties within the real estate ecosystem. This system tracks property conditions, maintenance history, and overall health metrics to ensure optimal property value and livability.

## Core Components

### 1. Property Health Metrics
- **Structural Integrity Score**: Assessment of foundation, walls, roof, and overall structural soundness
- **System Functionality**: Evaluation of electrical, plumbing, HVAC, and other essential systems
- **Maintenance History**: Record of all repairs, upgrades, and preventive maintenance
- **Environmental Factors**: Indoor air quality, presence of hazards (asbestos, lead, mold)
- **Energy Efficiency Rating**: Assessment of energy consumption and efficiency improvements
- **Safety Features**: Fire safety, security systems, accessibility compliance

### 2. Health Monitoring Categories

#### Structural Health (Weight: 30%)
- Foundation stability and integrity
- Roof condition and leak prevention
- Wall and flooring assessment
- Window and door functionality
- Overall structural soundness

#### System Health (Weight: 25%)
- Electrical system safety and capacity
- Plumbing functionality and water quality
- HVAC performance and efficiency
- Appliance condition and functionality
- Technology infrastructure (internet, security)

#### Environmental Health (Weight: 20%)
- Air quality measurements
- Water quality assessments
- Presence of environmental hazards
- Natural light availability
- Noise level assessments

#### Maintenance Health (Weight: 15%)
- Preventive maintenance schedule adherence
- Repair history and response time
- Upgrade frequency and quality
- Documentation completeness
- Warranty and guarantee status

#### Location Health (Weight: 10%)
- Neighborhood safety metrics
- Access to healthcare facilities
- Environmental quality of area
- Infrastructure reliability
- Future development plans

### 3. Health Scoring System

#### Rating Scale
- **Excellent (90-100)**: Property in pristine condition with recent upgrades
- **Good (75-89)**: Well-maintained with minor wear and tear
- **Fair (60-74)**: Some maintenance needed but structurally sound
- **Poor (40-59)**: Significant maintenance or repair required
- **Critical (0-39)**: Major issues requiring immediate attention

#### Health Report Format
```
Property Health Report: [Property ID]
Generated: [Date]
Overall Health Score: XX/100 ([Rating])

Category Scores:
- Structural Health: XX/100
- System Health: XX/100
- Environmental Health: XX/100
- Maintenance Health: XX/100
- Location Health: XX/100

Recommendations:
1. [Priority 1 items]
2. [Priority 2 items]
3. [Priority 3 items]

Next Review Date: [Date]
```

### 4. Maintenance Tracking

#### Scheduled Maintenance Items
- HVAC filter replacement: Every 3 months
- Plumbing inspection: Annually
- Electrical system check: Every 2-3 years
- Roof inspection: Annually
- Exterior maintenance: Seasonally
- Safety equipment checks: Monthly

#### Urgent Alerts
- System failures requiring immediate attention
- Safety hazards discovered during inspection
- Environmental concerns needing quick resolution
- Structural issues that could worsen rapidly

### 5. Health Improvement Recommendations

#### Short-term Actions (0-6 months)
- Immediate repairs and maintenance
- Safety improvements
- Energy efficiency upgrades
- Cosmetic improvements

#### Medium-term Improvements (6-18 months)
- System upgrades
- Structural enhancements
- Environmental improvements
- Technology integration

#### Long-term Planning (18+ months)
- Major renovations
- Energy efficiency projects
- Accessibility improvements
- Value-addition projects
